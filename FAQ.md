### 6. How accurate is the calculator?

**Short answer:** As accurate as your inputs.

**The math is precise:**
- Basic runway: `Savings / (Expenses - Income)`
- FIRE calculations: Industry-standard 4% rule
- Investment returns: Compound interest formula
- Phase planning: Month-by-month projection

**But accuracy depends on YOU:**

**‚úÖ Accurate inputs = Accurate results:**
- Realistic expense estimates
- Conservative income projections
- Regular updates (monthly)

**‚ùå Garbage in = Garbage out:**
- Underestimating expenses (common!)
- Overestimating side income
- Forgetting irregular costs (car repair, medical)

**Pro tips for accuracy:**
1. **Overestimate expenses by 10-15%** (buffer for surprises)
2. **Underestimate income by 20-30%** (freelance is unpredictable)
3. **Review bank statements** (don't guess!)
4. **Update monthly** (life changes)

### 7. What counts as "savings"?

**‚úÖ Include:**
- Checking account balance
- Savings account balance
- Money market accounts
- CDs (if accessible without penalty)
- Cash (yes, literal cash)
- Liquid brokerage accounts (non-retirement)

**‚ùå Don't include:**
- Retirement accounts (401k, IRA, Roth IRA)
  - Why: Early withdrawal = penalties + taxes
  - Exception: If you plan to use Rule 72(t), ask an advisor
- Home equity
  - Why: Not liquid
  - Exception: HELOC (home equity line of credit) - maybe, but risky
- Investment properties
- Anything you can't access in <30 days

**Gray area:**
- **Emergency fund:** Include it, but mentally note it's your safety net
- **Crypto:** Include if you'd sell it for living expenses (volatile!)
- **Stocks:** Include if you'd liquidate (but consider taxes)

**Example calculation:**
```
Checking: $15,000 ‚úÖ
Savings: $30,000 ‚úÖ
401k: $100,000 ‚ùå (don't include)
Brokerage: $10,000 ‚úÖ (taxable account)
Crypto: $5,000 ‚úÖ (if you'd sell)
Emergency fund: $15,000 ‚úÖ (but keep as buffer)

Total to enter: $60,000
(Or $45,000 if excluding emergency fund)
```

**Conservative approach:** Exclude emergency fund. Better safe than sorry.

### 9. How do I estimate monthly expenses?

**Method 1: Review bank statements (most accurate)**

**Steps:**
1. Export last 3-6 months of transactions
2. Categorize expenses:
   - Rent/mortgage
   - Utilities (electric, water, internet)
   - Food (groceries + eating out)
   - Transportation (car, gas, insurance, Uber)
   - Insurance (health, car, home)
   - Subscriptions (Netflix, Spotify, gym)
   - Shopping (clothes, Amazon, etc.)
   - Entertainment (movies, bars, hobbies)
   - Miscellaneous (everything else)
3. Average monthly totals
4. Add 10-15% buffer

**Example:**
```
Rent: $1,500
Utilities: $200
Food: $600
Transport: $300
Insurance: $350
Subscriptions: $100
Shopping: $250
Entertainment: $200
Misc: $200
-----------
Total: $3,700
Buffer (10%): $370
-----------
Enter: $4,070/mo
```

---

**Method 2: Quick estimation (less accurate)**

Use this formula:
```
Monthly Expenses = 
  Fixed (rent, insurance) + 
  Variable (food, fun) + 
  Buffer (10%)
```

**Example:**
```
Fixed: $2,000 (rent $1,500 + insurance $500)
Variable: $1,000 (food, transport, fun)
Buffer: $300
Total: $3,300/mo
```

---

**Common mistakes:**

**‚ùå Forgetting irregular expenses:**
- Annual subscriptions (Amazon Prime)
- Car registration, maintenance
- Medical/dental (co-pays, meds)
- Gifts (birthdays, holidays)
- Travel

**‚úÖ Fix:** Add them!
```
Annual irregular: $3,600
Monthly equivalent: $300/mo
Add to your total
```

**‚ùå Using "ideal" budget:**
- "I should spend $2K/mo" (but actually spend $3.5K)

**‚úÖ Fix:** Use reality, not ideals.

---

**Pro tips:**

1. **Use budgeting app for 1 month:** Mint, YNAB, etc. ‚Üí Get accurate data
2. **Separate "employed" vs "unemployed" budgets:**
   - Employed: $3,500/mo (eating out, commuting, work clothes)
   - Unemployed: $2,800/mo (cook at home, no commute, casual clothes)
3. **Create 2 scenarios:** Realistic vs Lean

### 10. What about taxes?

**Good question!** Taxes depend on your income sources.

**If you have NO income (living off savings):**
- ‚ùå No taxes on withdrawals from savings accounts
- You're just spending your own money

**If you have side income (freelance, consulting):**
- ‚úÖ Taxes apply!
- Estimate: 25-30% (US federal + state + self-employment tax)
- **Important:** Enter AFTER-TAX income in calculator

**Example:**
```
Freelance income (gross): $2,000/mo
Taxes (30%): -$600
After-tax: $1,400/mo
Enter: $1,400
```

**If you sell investments (stocks, crypto):**
- Capital gains tax applies
- Short-term (<1 year): Ordinary income tax rate
- Long-term (>1 year): 0-20% (depending on income)

- ‚úÖ Always enter after-tax numbers

### 13. What is the FIRE Calculator?

**FIRE = Financial Independence, Retire Early**

Our FIRE Calculator has 4 tools:

**1. FI Number** (How much you need)
- Formula: Annual Expenses / 0.04 (4% rule)
- Example: $48K/year expenses ‚Üí $1.2M FI Number

- Factors: Current savings + monthly savings + investment returns

**3. Coast FIRE** (When you can stop saving)
- Amount that grows to FI Number by retirement age
- Example: Age 35, need $1.2M at 65 ‚Üí Coast = $200K

**4. Progress Tracking** (Visual journey)

## Troubleshooting

### 26. The calculator shows negative months

**What it means:**
Your expenses exceed your savings immediately.

**Example:**
```
Savings: $5,000
Monthly expenses: $3,000
Monthly income: $0
Result: 1.67 months (almost negative)
```

**Solutions:**

**1. Increase savings:**
- Save more before quitting
- Target: 6-12 months runway minimum

**2. Reduce expenses:**

### 27. My scenarios aren't saving

- Solution: Use cloud sync (requires account)

- Solution: Use normal browser window

- Solution: Try different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari)

**How to prevent:**
1. Create an account
2. Log in before creating scenarios
3. Enable cloud sync (Settings ‚Üí Data)
